Bio

  I was born in Southern California and raised by my mother and father. Though their relationship was short lived I was able to appreciate technology through, both of their unique perspectives. My mother was studying to be a digital artist and my father was an electrical engineer. Both were starting off their careers in the burgeoning field of computers while doing non-conventional ways. Though I did not understand the technology and code behind what I was viewing then, these early impressions formed by how my parents interacted with their work had a large part to play in my field of interest.

 I started programming in high school because of the wide breadth of opportunities it had to offer. I was new to the area at the time needed time to adjust. Fortunately, my quiet nature and intense personal drive to problem solve gave me a reliable way of feeling good about my work. Though I was not always the best student, by putting in just a bit more time and effort I could always manage to get the project done and have something to show as my own. This perseverance would eventually land me my first internship at Quicksilver software where I learned to apply my learned experiences into practical work. It was invaluable to my learning experience at it both humbled my achievements and brought weight to my work.

  From high school onwards college has been interesting, classes were of course more difficult but what I was learning was so much more rewarding. I took every opportunity I could but learned to balance my capabilities and time. Eventually, I earned a spot as secretary among Engineers in Medicine and Transferred to the college of Engineering at CU Boulder. Both have helped me network extensively and I hope to do more moving forward.
